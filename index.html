Undiagnosed ADHD In women

Shining light on women with ADHD.

What is ADHD

ADHD is a neurological disorder characterized by a persistent pattern of inattention - with or without hyperactivity and impulsivity - that interferes with daily functioning.

Potential Risks

untreated ADHD are at risk for low self-esteem, underachievement and problems like depression and anxiety

Lasting Effects

Symptoms of inattention and hyperactivity are present since childhood.

Current Effects

Having ADHD can cause failures in communication (words get scrambled or they can interrupt people without thinking about it)

Society Impact

Gender norms may force girls to mask and hide symptoms of ADHD. Stereotypes around neatness, organization, cooperation, compliance, and social behaviors may encourage girls and women to deny or compensate for ADHD symptoms in classrooms and family structures.

Effects of Society Impact

Because symptoms can be more subtle in girls, healthcare practitioners may be less likely to diagnose girls with ADHD unless they also show symptoms of emotional disorders

Future Effects

Multiple studies of girls with ADHD have shown that their self-esteem is often lower than boys with ADHD — even well into adulthood. They are also more likely to experience major psychological distress such as severe anxiety and depression. Chronic stress due to these issues can also lead to other conditions, such as fibromyalgia.

If you feel like you’re overwhelmed during a conversation
  • Try to breathe in slowly and fully exhale
  • Try to mentally rehearse Instead of interrupting
  • If you catch yourself interrupting, own up to it. Say, “I’m sorry to interrupt. What were you going to say?”
If you struggle with school because you find yourself daydreaming a lot, you think the class is boring, or you struggle to stay seated, focus on your strengths instead of your weaknesses
  • Rewrite notes, take frequent breaks, highlight or color code important information, and find a quiet place that is used for studying only
  • Develop a regular routine
Tips to avoid perfectionism
  • Set realistic and attainable goals
  • Acknowledge that everyone makes mistakes
  • Focus on one thing at a time
  • Believe in yourself and your accomplishments
  • Break overwhelming tasks into smaller ones
Tips to handle friendships better
  • Let your friends know form time to time about how much they mean to you
  • Make plans as often as possible
  • Try deciding how much you value your friendships
  • Try to be a better listener, especially when you find yourself interrupting a lot
Dealing with procrastivity
  • Reward yourself after each completed task
  • Use time management techniques
  • Confront your avoidance
Working with mood swings
  • Work out to let your frustration out
  • Try yoga and meditation
  • Write down your feelings
  • Go outside more and exploring calming natural spots
The best free ADHD apps
  • Brain Focus- Blocks notifications and apps so phone distractions are not an option when you need to focus
  • Remember the milk- When tasks feel overwhelming, use this app to create task lists with their own subtasks
  • Mint- Helps keep track of finances like bank account balances, investments, credit score, and debts
  • TODOIST- Get things out of your head and onto a list that stays with you, pings you, and motivates you to be productive
