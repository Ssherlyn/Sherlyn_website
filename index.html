Undiagnosed ADHD In women

Shining light on women with ADHD.

What is ADHD

ADHD is a neurological disorder characterized by a persistent pattern of inattention - with or without hyperactivity and impulsivity - that interferes with daily functioning.

Potential Risks

untreated ADHD are at risk for low self-esteem, underachievement and problems like depression and anxiety

Lasting Effects

Symptoms of inattention and hyperactivity are present since childhood.

Current Effects

Having ADHD can cause failures in communication (words get scrambled or they can interrupt people without thinking about it)

Society Impact

Gender norms may force girls to mask and hide symptoms of ADHD. Stereotypes around neatness, organization, cooperation, compliance, and social behaviors may encourage girls and women to deny or compensate for ADHD symptoms in classrooms and family structures.

Effects of Society Impact

Because symptoms can be more subtle in girls, healthcare practitioners may be less likely to diagnose girls with ADHD unless they also show symptoms of emotional disorders

Future Effects

Multiple studies of girls with ADHD have shown that their self-esteem is often lower than boys with ADHD — even well into adulthood. They are also more likely to experience major psychological distress such as severe anxiety and depression. Chronic stress due to these issues can also lead to other conditions, such as fibromyalgia.

Hyperactive-Impulsive Type ADHD

Combined Type ADHD

This is the most common type of ADHD. People with it have symptoms of both inattentive and hyperactive-impulsive types.
